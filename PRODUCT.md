# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

High-school students applying to college and their parents, addressed as equals. The student is the one whose story gets told and who does the work; the parent is often the one researching, vetting, and paying. Both land on the same pages: sections need story-warmth that speaks to a 16-18 year old and the credibility signals (credentials, outcomes, process) a parent scans for. Context: they arrive stressed by deadlines and the fear of the blank page, usually via referral or search.

## Product Purpose

The marketing site for Essays in Bloom, Alison Bloomfield's college-application essay coaching practice (1:1 coaching, workshops, application strategy). It exists to turn visits into booked free consults. Success is a visitor trusting Alison enough to get on her calendar.

## Positioning

A teacher's method and a personal touch: a real human with real classroom credentials, not a service or a template.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Book a free consult. Secondary: How it works (the approach page) and Read more stories (testimonials) for visitors not ready to commit.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "What's your story?"
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real, experienced teacher, not an essay mill; (2) her process keeps the essay in the student's own voice; (3) it works, students landed where they belonged; (4) starting is easy and free.
- Proof on hand: real student and parent testimonials (`Website/src/data/testimonials.ts`), the school logo wall (`Website/src/data/schools.ts`), and Alison's credentials list (About page).

## Brand Personality

Warm, tactile, playful-premium. A crafted, cozy confidence: the site should feel like it was made by hand by someone who loves writing, with moments of play (the blooming logo, the font-flipping "story") that reward attention without undercutting the premium price of the service.

## Anti-references

- Cutesy or juvenile: no clip-art whimsy, pastel kids-brand energy, or decoration that makes a premium service feel like a craft fair.
- Generic AI landing page: no template SaaS grammar (eyebrow-heading-card-grid repeated per section), stock gradients, or interchangeable sections that could belong to any brand.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** A site selling great writing must itself read and feel crafted: copy, type, and detail are the proof of competence.
2. **A real human, visibly.** Alison's presence (photo, credentials, voice) beats abstractions; the personal touch is the differentiator, so show the person.
3. **Play earns its place.** Signature moments (bloom, pen pop, story flip) are rewards for attention, never noise; each one should feel authored, not decorated.
4. **The student's voice is the star.** Proof leads with students' own words and outcomes; the brand frames the story, it never talks over it.
5. **One page, two readers.** Every section carries both the emotional read (student) and the credibility read (parent) without splitting into separate tracks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: 4.5:1 text contrast (3:1 for large text), full keyboard operability with visible focus states, and `prefers-reduced-motion` respected everywhere (already a hard rule in `Website/DESIGN.md`). Light Bronze/Eggshell and Periwinkle are decorative-only colors, never text on light backgrounds.
